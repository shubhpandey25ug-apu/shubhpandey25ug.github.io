# The Presentations
**Considerations for the Information Scientist**, Unit 5. This unit had only one presentation. We were provided by the topics of our groups, and guidelines for the same. The groups were the same as what we had for the failure Wiki assessment. Till this time, we had better coordination among ourselves and have developed some confidence.  
Even though my group’s assigned topic was something about _Inclusive Terminologies_ in tech (which is quite interesting and something needed to be considered as being an Information Scientist), I would like to talk about the presentation given by** group 4**, with the title **_“Coding Against the Odds”_**.  The group tried to talk about how the tech field is so called dominated by male even though there were many female computer scientists from a very early period of time and they have contributed significantly. There was a good long conversation between students of the group, the instructor and the other students of class about this. One of my groupmate asked the question why the achievements made by women aren't as popular as those made by men or say they are not encouraged in society in any of the fields. One of the group members answered the question very confidently and talked about other factors that likely discourage such achievements as the dominance of male in overall society.  
The best part I liked about the presentation was that the group talked about how _there is a good increase in the number of females in the tech sector in India._  
Our instructor also shared her own lived experience which was very unlikely of her (at least I think so). She told us about how at the start we could feel like we aren't made for computer science but once we start giving constant efforts and work hard to understand things, we can do computing and programming for sure to great extents. She gave her own example about how she herself faced problems at the very beginning. She also stated that _women tend to take lesser risks or say they are more hesitant/more conscious._
